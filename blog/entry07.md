## Context
Upon finishing our freedom project, we did two different types of presentations, our in class presentation, as well as our expo elevator pitch. If im being totally honest, I don't think I did very well on either of them, I was unsure of what to say and was lost most of the time. Despite this, I do believe I have gotten some takeaways from both presentations.
### Expo Elevator Pitch
A takeaway from the Expo was definetly to keep it simple, I overcomplicated what I was saying too judges who were most likely expecting a short brief summary of what I had to present but I didn't come up with a proper way of saying everything I wanted to say in that small time frame. I think next time I could better plan my elevator pitch, going in I had a fond idea of what to say but switched it up everytime I presnted to someone new which was ineffective with finding the correct way to say something.
### In Class [Presentation](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1pP_gzBrD9EI8o4TyXSexrVWhwO7Kz3qC1SPM51ACz5I/edit?usp=sharing)

I was actually sick for some time and excused from presentation and without me knowing it was to a point that it skipped my placement. I think definetly think that my presentation could've gone a lot smoother if I knew what to say especially since I forgot to look over my [presentation plan](https://github.com/hensons6240/sep10-freedom-project/blob/main/prep/presentation.md).
